The Making

From scrap to
story

01

Salvage
the material

Old bolts, bent rebar, spent springs, socket heads, coil wire. Nothing is bought new — every piece starts as something discarded.

02

Find
the figure

Before a weld is laid, the character is spotted in the material. The shape of a bolt suggests a torso. A coil becomes a spine. The sculptor sees people in iron.

03

Weld &
compose

Each joint is hand-welded. Proportions are eyeballed, not measured. The result is never perfectly symmetrical — and that's exactly right.

04

Finish &
release

A heat patina, brushed finish, or matte black coat. Then a plant, a stone, or a bottle is introduced — and the character comes alive.
